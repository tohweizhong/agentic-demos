#### **Conflict Title**: Inconsistent Guidance on Mobile Accessibility Strategy

*   **Conflicting Files**:
    *   `GovTech Products and Services Apr26/Digital Service Standards/Baseline Design Practices.pdf`
    *   `GovTech Products and Services Apr26/Digital Service Standards/Digital Service Standards (DSS) _ Government Technology Agency of Singapore (GovTech Singapore).pdf`
    *   `GovTech Products and Services Apr26/Accessibility/Accessibility _ Government Technology Agency of Singapore (GovTech Singapore).pdf`
    *   `GovTech Products and Services Apr26/Digital Service Standards/WCAG _ Operable.pdf`
    *   `GovTech Products and Services Apr26/Digital Service Standards/WCAG _ Perceivable.pdf`
    *   `GovTech Products and Services Apr26/Digital Service Standards/WCAG _ Robust.pdf`
    *   `GovTech Products and Services Apr26/Digital Service Standards/WCAG _ Understandable.pdf`

*   **The Conflict**:
    *   `Baseline Design Practices.pdf` states: "**If specific content is deemed unsuitable for mobile devices, disable mobile device access and clearly explain how it can be accessed.**"
    *   In contrast, `Digital Service Standards (DSS) _ Government Technology Agency of Singapore (GovTech Singapore).pdf` mandates: "**Implement Responsive Web Design techniques to ensure web-based digital services are optimised for various devices and screen sizes.**"
    *   Further reinforcing this, `Accessibility _ Government Technology Agency of Singapore (GovTech Singapore).pdf` champions: "**increasing digital inclusion by helping developers ensure websites and digital products meet accessibility standards for persons with disabilities (PWD).**" The various WCAG documents (`WCAG _ Operable.pdf`, `WCAG _ Perceivable.pdf`, `WCAG _ Robust.pdf`, `WCAG _ Understandable.pdf`) provide detailed guidelines aimed at universal access and usability regardless of device or assistive technology.
    *   Disabling mobile access for content directly contradicts the principle of responsive design, which aims to optimize content for *all* screen sizes, and the broader goal of digital inclusion and accessibility for Persons With Disabilities (PWDs) as championed by GovTech and detailed in WCAG guidelines. Restricting access goes against making content universally available and usable.

*   **Recommended Action**:
    The organization should clarify its official stance on mobile access for digital services. The `Baseline Design Practices.pdf` statement to "disable mobile device access" should be reviewed and likely revised or removed, aligning with the "Responsive Web Design" mandate from `Digital Service Standards` and GovTech's commitment to "digital inclusion" and WCAG compliance. Best practice dictates ensuring content is accessible and usable across *all* devices, rather than restricting access. If content genuinely cannot be made mobile-friendly, alternative accessible solutions or clear justifications for critical exceptions should be provided, not a blanket disablement.

#### **Conflict Title**: Discrepancy in Official Status and Mandate of LLM Cybersecurity Playbook

*   **Conflicting Files**:
    *   `GovTech Products and Services Apr26/Cybersecurity/Cybersecurity_Playbook_for_Large_Language_Model_LLM_Applications.pdf`
    *   `GovTech Products and Services Apr26/Data and AI/Data and AI _ Government Technology Agency of Singapore (GovTech Singapore).pdf`

*   **The Conflict**:
    *   `Cybersecurity_Playbook_for_Large_Language_Model_LLM_Applications.pdf` states: "**This playbook is a tentative draft. Adoption of this playbook by agencies is not obligatory.**"
    *   In contrast, `Data and AI _ Government Technology Agency of Singapore (GovTech Singapore).pdf` states: "**GovTech has produced an LLM Cybersecurity Playbook to guide agencies and industries in developing, procuring, deploying, and using LLMs with security in mind. GovTech commits to regularly reviewing the LLM Cybersecurity Playbook in consultation with AI practitioners to ensure its ongoing relevance.**"
    *   The first document presents the playbook as an early-stage, non-binding document ("tentative draft," "not obligatory"). The second document describes it as a "produced" guide that GovTech is actively maintaining and reviewing for "ongoing relevance," implying it is an established and authoritative guidance document expected to be used by agencies. This creates a significant inconsistency regarding the playbook's official status, its maturity, and whether agencies are expected to adhere to it.

*   **Recommended Action**:
    GovTech must immediately clarify the current and intended status of the LLM Cybersecurity Playbook. This involves updating one or both documents to reflect a singular, consistent message regarding whether the playbook is still a draft, a finalized but optional guide, or a mandated standard. If it is intended to be a foundational guide, the "tentative draft" and "not obligatory" statements should be removed, and its official versioning and applicability clearly stated.
